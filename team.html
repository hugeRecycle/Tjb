PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR
Jianbo Tang, Ph.D.
Group leader

School of Engineering Material Science and Engineering

Jianbo Tang, from Luzhou, Sichuan, holds a bachelor's from Xi'an Jiaotong Univ. and a doctorate from Univ. of Chinese Academy of Sciences. He did post - doc research at Tsinghua Univ. and Univ. of New South Wales (UNSW), Australia, and was later hired as a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) at UNSW's Faculty of Engineering.
With a multidisciplinary background in material science, engineering thermophysics, mechanical manufacturing, and nanotechnology, he focuses on the structure - property - function correlations of materials at different scales and their design rules, applying these to innovative material research. Currently, his work centers on liquid metal material science.
He has received the Discovery Early Career Researcher Award for young talents in Australia. Having published over 80 peer - reviewed papers, with over 3800 citations and an h - index of 35, he has published representative works as first or corresponding author in top journals like Science, Science Advances, Nature Nanotechnology, etc.
In autumn 2024, Tang will join Westlake Univ.'s School of Engineering as an Assistant Professor (Distinguished Research Fellow) to set up the Laboratory of New Phase Metals and Manufacturing.
