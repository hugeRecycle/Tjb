Welcome to Research Theme 1!

The inquiry central to our research is: What does a disordered yet dynamic state of metallic matter imply from the perspective of a material scientist and engineer?

In finding the answers, we delve into a number of fundamental topics unique to liquid metals. These include atomic-scale events, microscopic structural evolutions, non-conventional phase transitions, surface/interface processes and effects, multi-component interactions, as well as multi-material systems involving liquid metals. By fusing interdisciplinary concepts and methodologies, we set out to discover innovative ways of producing high-value metals, alloys, metal derivatives (e.g., metal oxides), heterostructures, and composite materials with well-defined compositions, structures, properties and functions. A step beyond material design, our interest extends to integrating these material building blocks into devices and systems for applications in catalysis, (bio)sensing, electronics, optoelectronics, and thermal engineering settings.
